Microsoft Internship 2017: Interview Experience

I received Offer from Microsoft to intern at Microsoft IDC, Hyderabad. Microsoft visits our campus every on day 0. They are the first company to offer internships at my college. They kept a cut off of 7.0 GPA and only students from Computer Science and Engineering (CSE) and Information Technology (IT) were allowed for internships.

Online Coding Test

People who cleared the cut offs had to go through a Coding test. Coding round consisted of 3 Algorithms and Data structure problems. We were supposed to code in an IDE (forgot the name) where no other libraries like Standard Template library (STL) could be used and only C and C++ were allowed programming languages. All the questions were very easy questions. First question was a basic Implementation question. A brute force solution passed. Second question was an algorithmic question. The question gave large inputs and we had to return the product of them in an array. // Give a code snippet for the same. Third question was a Data Structures question. Given the root of a Binary Tree (whose structure was already given), we need to return true or false depending on whether the Binary Tree is a balanced Binary Search Tree or not. // Give a code snippet for the same. I solved all the questions with in 25 minutes.

Paper Coding round

Many student who had solved 2 questions sorted by time were shortlisted for a Paper Coding round. Students who came first and second in the Online coding round were given pass to directly interview without the need for a Paper Coding round. I was ranked 1 in the Online coding round and so I could skip the Paper Coding round. Even though I skipped the Paper coding round, I was curious to know the questions they asked in the Paper Coding round. They had asked 2 questions. One of the was a Algorithms Problem where a sorted array was given as input and we need to print all the pairs whose sum is X (again given as input). Second problem was a testing Problem where given a problem statement, how would one provide test cases to check the correctness. Microsoft people were very strict about the first problem. They considered both Time complexity and Memory complexity and also the coding style for shortlisting students. Students who gave a O(n) Time complexity and O(n) Memory complexity (Hash maps) solution were rejected. Second problem being an open ended problem, I didn't come across anyone who gave the best solution for the first problem but gave only few test cases for the second.

Technical Interviews

Performing the best in the Online round had both advantages and disadvantages. Advantage: I get to sit for interview without giving a Paper coding round. Disadvantage: I was the first person to go for the interviews in that placements session. My interview was scheduled at 7:30 AM. I got up at 6:45 AM took bath and went for the interview wearing a semi formal Shirt, Jeans pant and Sport Shoes :P . Microsoft is not very strict about dressing. My first Interview was one of the core members of Microsoft IDC. He asked me about my interests, my past experiences and past projects. I told him about my Internship and how I took my many responsibilities during the internship and about my interest in Databases, Backend and Infrastructure Programming and my past experiences in each of them. So he gave an open ended Design problem. The problem statement was to design a server which should handle multiple clients at once and should handle multiple updates which could be on the same entity and it should be notified to all the clients before any further update happens. He offered me a pen and a paper. I took couple of minutes to think of an approach and while I was thinking, I was discussing about the same with him. He stopped me to ask few questions in between like which tool according to you could be the best for that step or what if some user updates it around the same time etc. I confidently gave all the answers. He looked impressed and shook my hand after he told me that I would be having my interview in 2 minutes. I went outside of the cabin and I was called by a member of the recruitment team in another cabin. My second interview was more of a DSA interview. My interviewer was a member of Bing team and was working for more than 10 years in that team. My first question was to find the middle element of a Linked List given the head of the Linked List. I told my interviewer that I had come across the same problem before. I gave him the brute force approach to find the number of nodes in the Linked List and then iterate to Length/2. Then gave the solution with single traversal. Before implementing the solution, I gave him the corner cases and he was Impressed by my approach. I implemented my solution. He wanted a code walk-through of my solution. After that he asked me few questions on Threads vs Process. Synchronization and other few Operating Systems Concepts. He was really Impressed at the end.

Results

I was pretty confident about my interviews, but sometime in the afternoon some people were called by mistake and were told that they would be having their third Interview round. It was by mistake and those students were sent back, but I didn't knew about this situation and was sad thinking I didn't make it through for the Third round of interviews. At around 5:00 pm, Results were declared and my name was on top of the Selected Intern list. I was called to the training and placement building of my college. Microsoft gave goodies for the selected interns. I was very happy after the results were announced.
